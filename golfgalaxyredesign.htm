Golf Galaxy Dashboard Redesign Concept

Client: Spec

When: July 2015

Where: Philadelphia, Pa

Platform: Adobe Illustrator

View Prototype

As a designer, I always want to improve things. I will take any opportunity to make something better, even if it doesnâ€™t directly impact me. This instinct of mine kicked in one afternoon while at Golf Galaxy, when I caught a glimpse of a dashboard employees use to manage orders. The UI was scattered and lacked order and hierarchy. There was much room for improvement. I set out to design an new version of this dashboard, with customer information integrated so sale associates could make informed recommendations based on the customers buying history and swing performance data gathered by the indoor driving range simulator.

A glimpse of a bad interface

I couldn't bare the thought that employees would have to use such an awful interface. The spacing was off, there were things everything. Absolutely no order. I snapped a photo and set out to make it better.

Sketching before software

Quickly fleshing out ideas with paper and marker helps me zero in good options, as well as find out the bad options. After sketching, I jumped into Illustrator to wireframe.

Establishing a grid

Based on my typography scale, I use a few blocks to create consistency throughout the design. Multiples of 14px were used for spacing and sizing.
