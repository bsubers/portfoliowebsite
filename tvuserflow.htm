Fios and Xfinity User Flow Study

Client: Self Initiated

When: February 2015

Skills: Information Architecture

Tool: Adobe Illustrator

I decided to map out the steps it takes to queue up a tv show (Workaholics) on Verizon Fios. I then did the same test on Comcast Xfinity. It’s an interesting interface, because there isn’t a keyboard involved. Typing is a last resort. I tried to find the most efficient path the user could take. I found shortcuts with no indication of affordance, redundant taxonomy, poor system interactions and many other pain points in these experiences.

Rough sketch of the Verizon Fios flow

As I clicked through the interface, I created a crude map of the architecture. I wanted to uncover relationships between different categories, multiple paths, and taxonomy.

Comcast Xfinity Taxonomy

Much like Comcast's customer service, Xfinity's taxonomy is very inefficient. By repeating HD after each alphabetical cluster, it creates a longer list, which is more difficult to consume and comprehend at a glance.
